Helpful Studies and Articles

Alkhouri (2024)

This article explores how faith-based psychological beliefs support ethical decisions and stress mitigation in cybersecurity, connecting timeless spiritual practices with the digital age.

Ano & Vasconcelles (2005)

A meta-analysis of religious coping strategies, both positive and negative, and how they shape psychological resilience in high-stress environments like cybersecurity response.

Elia (2019)

Discusses ethical challenges and cyber threats in theological institutions, offering a unique lens on the intersection of theology and digital defense strategies.

Hollis (2023)

Based on interviews with 22 cyber professionals, this dissertation highlights the emotional and psychological toll of high-stakes cyber incidents, and the need for structured support systems.

Mott & Turner (2024)

First-hand accounts of cybersecurity responders describe PTSD-like symptoms and emotional strain following ransomware attacks, drawing attention to organizational healing strategies.

Oakley (2013)

Summarizes Serene Jones' work on trauma and grace, offering theological tools for healing and drawing parallels to the emotional recovery process for cyber responders.

Renaud & Dupuis (2023)

Explores religious values—believing, belonging, and doing—and their relevance to ethical conduct and mental well-being for cybersecurity professionals under pressure.
